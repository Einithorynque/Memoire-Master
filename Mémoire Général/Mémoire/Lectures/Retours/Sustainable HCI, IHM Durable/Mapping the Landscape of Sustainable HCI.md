## Résumé :

### Apparition des [[Sustainable HCI]]

« From the first appearance of two landmarks at CHI 2007 of what became the nascent subfield of “sustainable HCI” – the area has exploded. In early 2009, Goodman found 120 papers and grant abstracts related to the topic of the environment » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1975)

> [!important] Deux angles pour les SHCI
> - **In design** : durabilité du design (éco-conception, impact environnemental)
> - **Through design** : la durabilité transmise par le design (qui influencerait des modes de vies et décisions)
> 
> >« Mankoff et al. [49] offer a now widely adopted categorization of sustainable HCI into two orientations: sustainability in design (mitigating material effects of software/hardware), and sustainability through design (influencing sustainable lifestyles or decision-making). » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1976) sur « Mankoff et al. [49] »

### 5 Genres d'écrits sur le design durable
Les études sur le design durable sont rassemblées en 5 genres
#### Persuasive technology
**Persuader les utilisateurs était un sujet très discuté dans la recherche sur IHM durable**
> « Persuasion is a major theme in the sustainable HCI literature, comprising about 45% of our corpus » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

> « About 45% of these trace their theoretical rationale to BJ Fogg’s theory of persuasive technology » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Objectif : pousser les utilisateurs à agir de manière plus durable**
> « Within this genre, the standard approach is to design systems that attempt to convince users to behave in a more sustainable way. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Deux types de persuasion : appuyée et passive**
- appuyée : donner des informations sur leur comportement aux utilisateurs
- passive : des informations sur les impactes du comportement sont présentées
> (1) strong persuasion, in which information is provided about the extent to which a user’s behavior is or is not sustainable [e.g.,7], and 
> (2) passive persuasion, in which information about consumption, waste or other broad impact effects are presented to the users, usually implicitly contextualized within the topic of sustainability [e.g.,1,30,31] 
 (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

#### Ambient awareness
**Définition, encourager les comportements par des signes ambiant**
> « Ambient awareness systems draw upon the histories of calm computing and ambient displays to construct systems intended to make users aware of some aspect of the sustainability of their behavior, or qualities of the environment associated with issues of sustainability (about 25% of our corpus). » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Il y a une superposition entre le méthode de persuasion et de prise de conscience par l'ambiance**
> « There is a large overlap between ambient awareness and persuasive technology: many persuasive systems are ambient, based on the idea that the ambiently provided information will persuade the user to behave sustainably. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Les objets décrits prennent des formes très larges**
> « The forms of these systems range dramatically, from devices and physical artifacts [e.g.,30,31] to visualizations [e.g.,37] to instrumented environments [e.g.,5,16] and intelligent agents [e.g.,1] » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Deux techniques de design majoritairement employées**
- rendre la consommation de l'objet visible pour sensibiliser quant à leur utilisation
- rendre visible et récompensant les consommations désirables
> « two primary design tactics employed in this genre: the former makes consumption visible in order to prompt awareness of use [e.g.,5], while the latter makes visible (and aesthetically rewarding) desirable consumption patterns [e.g.,31]. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

#### Sustainable interaction design
**Le genre du design d'interaction durable désigne des papier utilisant la durabilité pour repenser le role du design**
> « Sustainable interaction design (SID)[11] describes papers oriented around using sustainability as a “critical lens” [34] to rethink the role and outcomes of design (ca. 10% in our corpus). » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Il se base sur la littérature et est souvent orienté vers la critique philosophique**
> « These works come out of and often speak to the design research literature, and are frequently philosophically and critically oriented. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Ces textes tendent à repenser fondamentalement la manière dont nous pensons l'interaction homme machine**
> « SID works tend to see a need to fundamentally rethink the methods of HCI in order address sustainability. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

> [!important] Ces travaux étaient souvent centré sur les effets matériels du numérique
> > « The work is often focused on material effects, i.e. reducing resource wastage and pollution, especially due to the rapid obsolescence of current technologies [e.g.,80]. Some papers address other issues such as encouraging mass transportation [67]. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

**Méthodologie :**
> « Methodologies in this genre vary, including conceptual design, critical design, thought pieces, and design criticism. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1977)

#### Formative user studies
**Ce genre désigne des études plus anthropologiques sur les utilisateurs**
>« This genre consists of studies to understand users’ attitudes to the environment or to (un)sustainable design (ca. 15%). » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

>« how users think about and approach sustainability as a first step to new design. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

**Ces travaux tendent à légitimer le fait que les changements de consommation dépendent énormément des contexte d'utilisation**
> « these works tend to legitimize differences in attitude towards sustainability and to show how individuals are embedded in social and cultural systems which constrain the potential sustainability of their behavior. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

#### Pervasive and Participatory Sensing
**Ce genre d'études tends à collecter des données à l'aide de non chercheur pour améliorer des outils non durables**
> « A significant emerging strand of work uses sensors to monitor and report on (usually adverse) environmental conditions, with the implicit goal of using the data collected to change these conditions (ca 22% of our corpus). » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)
> « many of these papers focus on participatory sensing, which generally means the involvement of non-experts in collecting data from sensing platforms. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)
> « For example, in the Cyclesense pilot project Biketastic (http://biketastic.com/), bicyclists are enrolled to collect data concerning road surface conditions in Los Angeles, using the built-in accelerometers and GPS services in mobile phones. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)







### Points problématiques 
Différences de points de vues, d'approches dans l'étude du design durable listé par l'étude 
#### Sustainability as research focus vs. application area.
Utiliser le design durable comme un secteur d'application pour étudier une autre discipline ou ancrer sa recherche dans la recherche d'un réel design écologie.
> « sustainability as a convenient application domain may address sustainability more superficially or even misaddress some issues (for example, by not considering the possible wastefulness of energy used by a game to teach people not to waste energy) » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

#### Individual consumers vs. other users, groups, or scales.
>« the bulk of papers in the corpus (about 70%) target users conceived of as individual consumers » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

> [!warning] Penser l'humain à l'échelle du groupe
> « Foth, Paulos, Satchell and Dourish [23,26] argue for the need to design for other ‘scales’ such as building affiliative groups or at the level of the nation-state, like designing for activist groups [29,61,66,78] or multiple stakeholders [4,57]. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

**Penser dans les rôles sociaux plutôt qu'en terme de consommateurs**
>« Another option is to keep the focus on individuals, but see them through the lens of other social roles. For example, participatory sensing and other communityoriented work [17,22,32,58] tend to see individuals as members of a democratic public, as citizens rather than as consumers, » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1978)

> [!important] Sortir de l'utilisation
> Bohlen and Tan and Kobayashi, Ueoka and Hirose [15,47] design to increase affective links to nature without reference to consumption.

#### Users as the problem vs. solving users’ problems.

> « Many of the papers in our corpus see user behavior as causing environmental problems and therefore in need of change. This orientation frequently arises in persuasive and ambient works, which aim to let users know which aspects of their behavior are environmentally problematic and/or prod them to change. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

> « Other papers, notably the formative studies, aim to drive design primarily from needs and opportunities raised by users, rather than seeing them as the problem. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)
> « In sustainable interaction design for example, unsustainable behavior is often seen as a problem caused not by bad users but by bad design, with the solution being better design options that will enable more positive behavior. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

#### Improving vs. fundamentally changing lifestyles.
Les approches dites de design d'interaction durable auraient plutôt tendance à vouloir changer fondamentalement les comportements, les pratiques.
>  Some of the papers in our corpus support current lifestyles while increasing their sustainability, for example by supporting existing activities while reducing their resource usage [e.g.,42]. Others, notably many in sustainable interaction design, emphasize the need for fundamental cultural change, rather than simply increasing the efficiency of current lifestyles [34]. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

#### Technology as an adequate vs. inadequate solution.
Les articles ont majoritairement tendance à utiliser les technologie comme une solution mais :

> « Yet others take a broader view of design involving not only the technology but also awareness of, and space for, non-technological or contextual issues: Wakkary and Wash [75,76] argue for designing for openness and reappropriation, Bohlen and Tan [15] limit their technology to leave space for more direct interaction with nature, and DiSalvo et al. [22] emphasize the importance of participatory, communal aspects of design over the ‘final product.’ » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

> « Kobayashi et al. [47], for example, point out the inherent contradiction in attempting to use technology to create more intimate connections with nature. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)
#### HCI as usual vs. HCI must be rethought.
> « The majority of papers in our corpus use existing HCI methods and orientations to approach problems of sustainability, but others argue that the structure of HCI as a field itself contributes to the problems of unsustainability. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

- Les standards de l'HCI pourrait amener à produire toujours plus 
- L'utilisation incite à ne plus utiliser

#### Political differences are relevant vs. irrelevant.
>« Issues around the environment are a source of great political debate in society. The overwhelming majority of research and design in sustainable HCI does not acknowledge or address such differences » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1979)

> « Woodruff et al. and Hank et al. [34,78] differentiate users’ orientations to sustainability and highlight how this leads to a need for different forms of design. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1980)

> « Nathan et al. [57] likewise recognize the politics inherent in design and planning, and propose a method for HCI researchers to work within the politics of a situation through the development of value-driven scenarios. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1980)



### Problématiques émergeantes 

#### Les articles tendent à se répéter
Point déjà confirmé par les recherches passées en 2010  : 
- Les produits doivent répondre concrètement à un besoin pour ne pas devenir obsolète
	- This leads to unaddressed challenges, such as how we might support users attached to software and hardware that has been declared obsolete by the industry.
- Il est établit que le design ambiant pourrait conforter des comportements écologistes 
	- Pas d'écrit sur les résultats cependant
- On sait qu'il faudrait s'adresser à des groupes d'utilisateurs, des masses (à l'échelle d'état, de région)
- Qu'il faut trouver autre chose que la morale des utilisateurs pour les inviter à un changement
- Nous abordons fréquemment la durabilité à travers la conception technologique, mais nous devons trouver des moyens de traiter les questions de politique.
- Les utilisateurs devraient devenir futur experts de la durabilité

#### La discipline s'isole 

> « Another area where connections wait to be built is to professional design. Although sustainable interaction design is building strong ties to the design research community, there is a significant gap between the professional fields of industrial and interaction design and design research in sustainable HCI. » (DiSalvo et al., 2010, p. 1980)

### Des débats sur la pratique n'ont pas lieu

- La technologie n'est pas une réponse neutre à la crise
- Les démarches persuasive pour orienter des comportements peuvent ne pas être éthique (et s'éloigner de l'idée d'un design centré utilisateur)
- Si nous convenons qu’un changement fondamental est nécessaire et que ce changement pourrait ne pas être voulu par les utilisateurs, qui décide de ce qui doit changer et comment ? Quels besoins sont satisfaits, et quelles valeurs comptent au final ?
